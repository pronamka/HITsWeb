ANT
ALGORITHM

Ant algorithm is one of the effective polynomial algorithms for finding approximate solutions
to the traveling salesman problem, as well as for solving similar problems of route finding on graphs. The essence of the approach is to analyze and use the model of ants' behavior, looking for paths
from a colony to a food source, and represents a metaheuristic optimization.

All choices are ranked according to their suitability. The amount of deposited pheromones
for each solution is weighted so that more suitable solutions receive more pheromones than less suitable ones.

Draw a maze, place food sources and colony. And watch the ants work!

Neural networks are powerful tools used for pattern recognition and classification. One common type, the multilayer perceptron, can be trained to recognize handwritten digits by learning from examples. It belongs to a broader class of machine learning models that approximate complex functions through layers of interconnected nodes.

The network processes input as a grid of pixel values and predicts the most likely digit. During training, the model adjusts internal weights to reduce prediction errors. The more examples it sees, the better it becomes at recognizing digits with high accuracy.

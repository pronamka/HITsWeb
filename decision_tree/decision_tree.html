Decision trees are intuitive and interpretable models used for classification and regression tasks. They work by recursively splitting data based on feature values, forming a tree-like structure where each leaf represents a final decision or prediction. This makes them ideal for understanding how decisions are made.

The tree is trained on labeled data, learning to divide information in a way that maximizes the distinction between different outcomes. Once trained, the model can take new input and follow the path of logical decisions down the tree to reach a prediction.

Upload your dataset, then try out your own inputs and watch the tree in action! As your data moves through each decision, the path to the final prediction lights up step by step.
